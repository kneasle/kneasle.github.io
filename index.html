Hello traveller!

You've stumbled across my website - what a great place to find yourself! This is where I gather all my projects, ideas, etc. in one place for anyone to peruse at their leisure. It is entirely my own work, from the content/projects right down to the icons and web design.

Let me introduce myself...

My name's Ben White-Horne and I'm starting my 4th year studying Computer Science at Oxford University (at St Anne's college, along with the other beavers). On the internet I'm usually known as 'Kneasle', and I sometimes livestream coding on my YouTube channel of the same name - you'd be very welcome to come and chill out while we build crazy projects.

This website is mainly devoted to programming in various languages (mostly Rust), but other than computing I usually occupy myself with change ringing, a mathematical and largely secular way of ringing church bells that is pretty much exclusive to England. Change ringing also features quite heavily in this site, since there is a nice amount of intersection between maths/computing and the theory of change ringing.

I also play trombone and drums and produce funky/dancy electronic music. My recent music is on Spotify (amongst other places). Earlier music remains on YouTube and SoundCloud as an archive for those who want to listen to it...
