Hello traveller!

My name's Ben White-Horne and I like making computers do interesting things quickly.

This is my personal website where I gather all my projects, ideas, etc. in one place for anyone to peruse at their leisure. It is entirely my own work, from the content/projects right down to the icons and web design.

The content in this site largely follows my personal interests and hobbies. It is mainly devoted to programming in various languages (mostly Rust), but other than computing I usually occupy myself with change ringing, a very mathematical and largely secular way of ringing church bells that is pretty much exclusive to England. Change ringing also features quite heavily in this site, since there is a nice amount of intersection between maths/computing and the theory of change ringing.

I currently work at MathWorks (of MATLAB fame), and before that I studied Computer Science at St Anne's College, Oxford (with the other beavers). On the internet I'm usually known as 'Kneasle', and I sometimes livestream coding on my YouTube channel of the same name - you'd be very welcome to come and chill out while we build crazy projects.

I also play trombone and drums and produce funky/dancy electronic music. My recent music is on Spotify (amongst other places). Earlier music remains on YouTube and SoundCloud as an archive for those who want to listen to it.
